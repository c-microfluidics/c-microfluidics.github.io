What is microfluidics?

Microfluidics is the study of systems able to process small quantities of fluids with the aid of channels, which measure from tens to hundreds of micrometers. This field has emerged in recent years as a new area of research, with applications in medicine, biology, chemistry, and physical sciences.

Microfluidic devices enable the manipulation of small volumes of fluid precisely, allowing to perform an analysis with limited quantities of samples, while it shortens analysis time, provides high resolution and sensitivity in detection or separation of molecules, and delivers faster results. By reducing the amount of reagents used, it creates a positive impact on the environment and a noticeable cost reduction.[1]
